About

Experienced teaching, with time to slow things down.

Maths can feel impossible when a student has missed a few building blocks or stopped believing they can do it.

My job is to find the sticking point, explain it clearly, and help them move forward without judgement.

I spent 16 years in the classroom, so I know that students rarely struggle because they are simply “bad at maths”. Often they need an explanation that makes sense to them and enough calm practice for it to stick.

Lessons are shaped around the individual student, whether they need to rebuild core skills, keep up with school, prepare for a GCSE resit, or work towards a stronger exam grade.

I keep the atmosphere friendly and focused. Parents can also have straightforward updates on what we are covering and where the next priorities are.

How lessons work

Clear support, one step at a time.

Every lesson starts from what the student can do now, not where a textbook says they should be.

1. Find the gaps

  • We begin with the topics that feel hardest right now.
  • I check the foundations underneath, without making it a test.
  • We agree on a sensible focus for the next few lessons.

2. Make it make sense

  • Ideas are broken into clear, manageable steps.
  • Students can ask questions without feeling rushed.
  • We use examples that build understanding, not guesswork.

3. Build confidence

  • Practice is chosen at the right level and gradually stretched.
  • Progress is noticed so students can see what has changed.
  • Exam technique is added when the underlying maths is secure.

Frequently asked questions

A few things parents usually want to know.

Who are lessons for?

GCSE students at any starting point, including those who feel anxious, have fallen behind, need a confidence boost, or want to aim for a higher grade.

How do online lessons work?

Lessons take place live online with a shared whiteboard, so the student can see each step, ask questions, and work through examples together with me.

Where are in-person lessons held?

In-person GCSE maths tuition is available in Hull by arrangement. Send your area and preferred times so I can confirm what is practical.

What happens in the trial lesson?

We look at what your child is finding difficult, work through some maths together, and decide whether the teaching style and lesson format feel like a good fit.

Can you help with exam preparation?

Yes. Lessons can cover topic gaps, problem solving, calculator and non-calculator skills, exam questions, timing, and the habits that help students show what they know.

Will parents receive updates?

Yes. Parent updates are available and can include the topics covered, progress noticed, and the areas that would be useful to work on next.
