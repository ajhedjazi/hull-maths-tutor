The real problem

Your child isn’t lazy — they’re probably stuck somewhere.

A missed step can quietly grow into a bigger problem. Fractions make algebra harder. Weak number sense slows every question down. One rushed lesson can turn into “I’m just bad at maths.”

The answer is not more random worksheets. It is finding the point where things stopped making sense, then rebuilding from there without judgement or hurry.

  • MethodSteps learned without understanding
  • PressureAnxiety when the clock starts
  • ConfidenceNot knowing where they went wrong

The HMT method

First we find the gap. Then we rebuild confidence.

Every stage has a purpose: understand the problem, restore the method, make it hold under exam pressure, and show parents what has changed.

Frequently asked questions

The practical details, clearly answered.

If your question is not here, include it in the enquiry form and I will reply personally.

Do you only teach GCSE maths?

The main focus is GCSE maths, including students rebuilding earlier foundations and post-16 students preparing for a resit.

Do you offer online lessons?

Yes. Online one-to-one lessons use shared working so each step can be explained, practised, and reviewed together.

Do you offer in-person lessons in Hull?

Yes, by arrangement at the student’s home or an agreed local location, depending on area and timetable.

What happens in the £20 trial lesson?

We look at where your child feels stuck, work through some maths together, and identify a sensible next step without pressure.

What if my child lacks confidence?

That is a common starting point. Lessons slow the method down, notice genuine progress, and build challenge gradually.

Do you support Foundation and Higher GCSE?

Yes. Support is shaped around the student’s current level, exam tier, topic gaps, and goals.

Are small groups available yet?

Not yet. Small teacher-led groups are planned for a later phase, and families can register their interest through the form.
