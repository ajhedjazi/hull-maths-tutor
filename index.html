Why this works

More questions only help once we know what needs fixing.

Most students do not need endless new questions. They need to understand their own working.

When a student gets a multi-step algebra, ratio or geometry question wrong, a typical tutor might simply reteach the topic. I ask: “Talk me through what you were thinking on this line.”

Usually, the student knows more than they realise. The issue is one missing step or hidden misunderstanding that keeps appearing across different questions.

Once the missing step is found, the confusion starts to make sense — and then we can fix it.

I don’t move on until we know where the method breaks down. Parents get a clear, honest summary of what I found and what I’d fix first.

Focused practice

Practice That Shows the Thinking

Many students are given more worksheets when they get stuck. More practice can help — but only once we know where the misunderstanding is.

That is why my worksheets are designed to reveal thinking, not just collect answers. Questions can be broken into smaller steps so I can see whether the issue is a step in the working, calculation, layout, vocabulary, reasoning or recall.

Instead of simply saying “wrong”, we can say:

  • “This step is secure.”
  • “This step needs work.”
  • “This is where the thinking goes off track.”
  • “Now let’s rebuild it.”

Frequently asked questions

The practical details, clearly answered.

If your question is not here, include it in the enquiry form and I will reply personally.

Do you only teach GCSE maths?

The main focus is GCSE maths, including students rebuilding earlier foundations and post-16 students preparing for a resit.

Do you offer online lessons?

Yes. Online one-to-one lessons use shared working so each step can be explained, practised, and reviewed together.

Do you offer in-person lessons in Hull?

Yes, by arrangement at the student’s home or an agreed local location, depending on area and timetable.

What happens in the £20 introductory session?

We work through carefully chosen questions, look at your child’s working line by line, and identify the first missing step I would fix.

What if my child lacks confidence?

That is a common starting point. Lessons slow things down, make progress visible, and rebuild challenge gradually from the step that needs attention.

Do you support Foundation and Higher GCSE?

Yes. Support is shaped around the student’s current level, exam tier, topic gaps, and goals.

Can tutoring align with school work?

Where appropriate, and with parent permission, I can contact school or review published curriculum information so tutoring supports what is happening in class.

Are small groups available yet?

Not yet. Small teacher-led groups are planned for a later phase, and families can register their interest through the form.
