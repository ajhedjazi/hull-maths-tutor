The foundation problem

More questions do not fix a broken foundation.

Most students do not need endless new questions. They need to understand their own working.

When a student gets a multi-step algebra, ratio or geometry question wrong, a typical tutor might simply reteach the topic. I ask: “Talk me through what you were thinking on this line.”

Usually, the student knows more than they realise. The issue is often a mix of topic gaps, confidence, routine, and the way they have learned to approach questions.

Once we understand what is really going on, the next step becomes calmer, clearer and much more specific.

Frequently asked questions

The practical details, clearly answered.

If your question is not here, include it in the enquiry form and I will reply personally.

Do you only teach GCSE maths?

The main focus is GCSE maths, including students rebuilding earlier foundations and post-16 students preparing for a resit.

Do you offer online lessons?

Yes. Some one-to-one GCSE maths tuition can be arranged online, using shared working so each step can be explained, practised, and reviewed together.

Do you offer in-person lessons in Hull?

Yes. The Maths MOT & Action Plan is an in-person diagnostic assessment, and regular tuition can also be arranged in person depending on area and timetable.

What happens in the £45 Maths MOT & Action Plan?

We work through carefully chosen questions in a relaxed way, look at your child’s working, and talk about what feels difficult. I identify strengths, gaps, confidence issues and how your child learns best, then give parents clear feedback and a personalised action plan.

What if my child lacks confidence?

That is a common starting point. I slow things down, make progress visible, and build challenge gradually so confidence can grow alongside the maths.

Do you support neurodivergent learners?

Yes. I support neurodivergent learners and understand that trust, routine, patience and confidence can make a real difference to how a student engages with maths.

Do you support Foundation and Higher GCSE?

Yes. Support is shaped around the student’s current level, exam tier, topic gaps, and goals.

Can tutoring align with school work?

Where appropriate, and with parent permission, I can contact school or review published curriculum information so tutoring supports what is happening in class.
