## Problem section

- The real problem
- You are not lazy. Your system is too fragile.
- Most people can start. The harder part is staying consistent when work gets heavy, sleep drops, family life takes over, an old injury flares up, or energy is lower than planned.
- The cycle is familiar: you start strong, miss a few sessions, feel like the week is ruined, then wait for Monday to begin again.
- That is not a character flaw. It is a plan that only works when life is perfect.
- Plans too rigid
- One missed workout becomes a broken week because the plan has no smaller gear.
- Progress too vague
- Without testing, tracking, and review, effort turns into guesswork.
- Life gets in the way
- Stress, travel, injuries, and low energy need contingencies, not guilt.

## All or Something section

- The operating principle
- All or Something
- All-or-nothing fitness creates big swings: perfect weeks followed by total drop-off.
- All-or-something fitness protects your baseline. Even on difficult days, you still do something useful.
- Fragile
- All or Nothing
- Perfect workout or no workout
- Perfect diet or write the day off
- Miss one session and restart on Monday
- Motivation-dependent
- Guilt-driven
- Resilient
- All or Something
- Full session or minimum effective dose
- Better choice instead of perfect choice
- Adjust the plan instead of abandoning it
- System-dependent
- Progress-driven
- The goal is simple: lower the variance, raise the baseline.

## Diagnostic onboarding

- Diagnostic onboarding
- Start with a clearer picture.
- The first phase is calm, structured, and practical. It turns your current body, schedule, limitations, and goals into a coaching blueprint you can actually use.
- 01
- Intake & Lifestyle Audit
- Clarify goals, schedule, stress, sleep, training history, and the barriers that usually get in the way.
- 02
- Bloodwork & Health Markers
- Use relevant markers to guide better lifestyle, training, and nutrition decisions.
- 03
- Fitness Test & Physio Screen
- Assess capacity, movement quality, restrictions, and injury considerations.
- 04
- Personal Blueprint
- Build training, nutrition, recovery, and habit targets around your real constraints.
- 05
- App Setup & Coaching Begins
- Track sessions, log progress, review adherence, and adjust the plan intelligently.
- Screen, test, then build
- Less guessing at the start means smarter adjustments once training begins.
- Bloodwork helps inform lifestyle, training, and nutrition decisions. Where medical interpretation or treatment is required, clients should consult an appropriate healthcare professional.
